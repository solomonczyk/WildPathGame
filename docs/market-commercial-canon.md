# Market and Commercial Canon

## Market and Competitor Analysis

WildPathGame competes for attention and spending against:

- free survival videos;
- emergency preparedness checklists;
- offline survival courses;
- mobile puzzle and hidden-object games;
- survival simulation games;
- first-aid and preparedness apps.

The product cannot win by volume of information. It wins by converting survival knowledge into short, memorable decision practice. The first market test should measure whether users will pay for confidence and structured practice, not merely say the idea is interesting.

## Primary Segment

Initial segment: Russian-speaking urban preparedness beginners. This segment is chosen because current product language and creator context are Russian-first, and the strongest pain is practical uncertainty rather than advanced fieldcraft.

Provisional decision: do not target professional rescuers, military audiences, or expert survivalists in MVP. Verification: interview experts for review, not as primary buyers.

## First Sellable Result

The first sellable result is a paid early-access mini-course:

Title: "First 72 Hours: Practical Survival Decisions"
Format: six browser episodes plus a printable evacuation checklist.
Promise: after completion, the learner can explain and practice the first-priority decisions for evacuation, water, warmth, injury, shelter, and human contact.

## Commercial Model

MVP model: one-time payment for early access.

Initial offers:

- Free demo: first apartment evacuation episode.
- Paid early access: six-episode mini-course.
- Instructor pilot: paid group demo and feedback call.

Do not introduce subscription until retention is proven by repeat scenarios or a content library.

## Pricing Hypotheses

- Individual early access: 9-19 USD equivalent.
- Family bundle: 29 USD equivalent after proof of family use.
- Instructor pilot: 99-249 USD equivalent for workshop use and feedback session.

Hypothesis validation: price anchor test on landing page and five paid pilot conversations.

## Free and Paid Boundaries

Free:

- first episode;
- basic explanation of the product;
- one practical checklist;
- waitlist or email capture.

Paid:

- full six-episode course;
- progress summary;
- printable pack list;
- instructor usage rights;
- future scenario packs.

## Unit Economics

MVP target:

- gross revenue per individual paid user: 9-19 USD equivalent;
- payment processing: 3-6%;
- AI image/content generation amortized under 0.50 USD per paid user by reusing assets;
- support time under 10 minutes per paid user during pilot;
- paid CAC target before scale: under 50% of purchase price.

Scale condition: paid acquisition is forbidden until organic or small paid tests show conversion from landing visit to paid user above 2%.

## Willingness-to-Pay Validation

Valid evidence:

- completed payment;
- signed paid pilot agreement;
- paid pre-order;
- explicit invoice request after demo.

Weak evidence:

- "I would use this";
- likes, comments, shares;
- unpaid waitlist alone.

Invalid evidence:

- friends praising the concept without payment action;
- survey answers without behavior;
- completion of free demo without next-step intent.

## First Sales Form

The first sale should be a paid pilot:

- target: 10 individual early-access buyers or 2 instructor buyers;
- payment: direct checkout or invoice;
- delivery: browser access plus checklist;
- promise: early access with manual support and feedback call option.

## Marketing Promise Boundaries

Allowed claims:

- "practice survival priorities";
- "learn decision-making through realistic scenarios";
- "build a better evacuation checklist";
- "not a replacement for certified training."

Forbidden claims:

- guaranteed survival;
- medical certification;
- professional rescue training;
- legal compliance for all regions;
- instruction to perform dangerous field actions without context.
