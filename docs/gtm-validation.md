# Marketing, Go-to-Market, and Customer Validation

## Acquisition Channels

Priority order:

1. Short video clips showing one survival mistake and consequence.
2. Survival and preparedness communities with transparent demo posts.
3. Creator-led build-in-public updates.
4. Partnerships with outdoor instructors after MVP.
5. Paid ads only after landing conversion is proven.

## Content Strategy

Content themes:

- "Would you take this item?"
- "Why expensive gear is not always survival gear."
- "First 72 hours: priority mistakes."
- "Hidden-object survival challenge."
- "What the game teaches after you fail."

Each content piece should drive to the free apartment episode or waitlist. Content must avoid fearmongering and unverifiable claims.

## Funnel

1. Content impression.
2. Landing page visit.
3. Free episode start.
4. Free episode completion.
5. Email capture or payment intent.
6. Paid early access.
7. Feedback interview.
8. Referral or testimonial.

## Landing

Landing page must show:

- one playable or recorded scene;
- clear statement: practical survival puzzle game;
- who it is for;
- what the first paid mini-course includes;
- safety disclaimer;
- price or pre-order option;
- waitlist fallback.

## Onboarding

Onboarding rule: no abstract tutorial. The user starts in the apartment scene and learns by selecting, packing, and seeing consequences. Controls are introduced as needed.

## Trial

Trial is the first episode. It must be polished enough to sell the paid course. It cannot feel like a wireframe.

## Beta

Closed beta target:

- 20 users;
- at least 12 complete the first episode;
- at least 8 complete three episodes;
- at least 5 provide written feedback.

## Paid Pilot

Pilot target:

- 10 individual buyers or 2 instructor buyers;
- payment before full course is expanded beyond six episodes;
- feedback collected through structured interview.

## Referral

Referral begins only after five paid users say they would recommend it. Early referral incentive: free future scenario pack for both inviter and invited buyer.

## Ad Launch Criteria

Paid advertising may start only when:

- landing-to-free-start conversion is at least 20%;
- free-start-to-completion is at least 45%;
- completion-to-paid-intent is at least 8%;
- no severe usability blocker remains;
- marketing promise is consistent with [Market and Commercial Canon](market-commercial-canon.md).

## Stop Conditions for Non-Working Channels

Stop a channel when:

- 300 qualified visits produce under 5% free episode starts;
- or 100 free starts produce under 2 paid-intent events;
- or comments show repeated misunderstanding of the promise;
- or acquisition cost exceeds target for two consecutive tests.

## Customer Validation

### Problem Interviews

Interview 15 target users. Valid evidence includes repeated stories of confusion, bad prioritization, or desire for structured practice. Invalid evidence is generic interest in survival.

### Message Test

Run three messages:

- "Practice the first 72 hours."
- "Survival decisions, not survival trivia."
- "A realistic evacuation puzzle."

Winner is selected by click-through and qualitative clarity, not preference alone.

### Landing Test

Minimum evidence: 300 qualified visits and at least 20 email signups or 6 paid-intent clicks.

### Waitlist

Waitlist is valid only if users choose a segment and answer why they joined.

### Concierge Prototype

Run guided sessions with five users, observing confusion and item priority. Do not explain unless they are blocked.

### Usability Test

Five first-time users must complete the apartment episode without verbal help.

### Learner Alpha

Alpha validates learning: users explain why water, warmth, light, first aid, documents, and knife matter after play.

### Closed Beta

Closed beta validates retention and course flow across at least three episodes.

### Paid Pilot

Paid pilot validates willingness to pay and delivery promise.

## Evidence Rules

Valid:

- observed behavior;
- payment;
- completion data;
- exact quotes tied to user segment;
- before/after explanation tests.

Invalid:

- unsegmented opinions;
- family encouragement;
- social likes without conversion;
- technical completion without learning evidence.

## GO / HOLD / PIVOT / STOP

GO: KPI threshold met and no zero-tolerance risk.
HOLD: evidence incomplete but promising.
PIVOT: segment, promise, or format fails while problem remains strong.
STOP: no payment intent after two validated messaging and prototype cycles.
